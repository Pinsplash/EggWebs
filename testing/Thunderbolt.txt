'''Thunderbolt''' (Japanese: '''ÔľĎÔľź„Āĺ„āď„Éú„Éę„Éą''' ''100,000 Volts'') is a damage-dealing {{type|Electric}} [[move]] introduced in [[Generation I]].

It is:
* [[TM24]] in Generation I, from [[Generation III]] to {{g|Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon}}, and in {{g|Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl}}.
* [[TM36]] in [[Pok√©mon: Let's Go, Pikachu! and Let's Go, Eevee!]].
* [[TR08]] in [[Pok√©mon Sword and Shield]].
* [[TM126]] in {{g|Scarlet and Violet}}.
* [[TM067]] in [[Pok√©mon Legends: Z-A]].

It can be upgraded to the special [[Z-Move]] {{m|Stoked Sparksurfer}} by {{rf|Alolan}} {{p|Raichu}} holding [[Aloraichium Z]], and to the special Z-Move {{m|10,000,000 Volt Thunderbolt}} by [[Pikachu in a cap]] holding [[Pikashunium Z]].

==Effect==
===Generation I===
Thunderbolt does damage and has a 10% chance of {{status|Paralysis|paralyzing}} the target. Thunderbolt cannot paralyze {{type|Electric}} Pok√©mon.

===Generation II to V===
Thunderbolt can now paralyze Electric-type Pok√©mon.

Thunderbolt can be used as the second move of a [[Contest combination|Pok√©mon Contest combination]], with the user gaining 4 bonus appeal points if {{m|Charge}} was used in the previous turn.

===Generation VI onwards===
Thunderbolt's power was decreased from 95 to 90. It can no longer paralyze {{type|Electric}} Pok√©mon once again, due to them now being immune to {{status|paralysis}}.

Thunderbolt can be used as part of a [[Contest combination|Contest Spectacular combination]], with the user gaining an extra three appeal points if {{m|Charge}} was used in the prior turn.

==Trivia==
* This move's name is the same as the {{pkmn|category}} of {{p|Electivire}} and {{p|Zebstrika}}.
* Thunderbolt is the only move that can be upgraded into multiple exclusive [[Z-Move]]s: {{rf|Alolan}} {{p|Raichu}}'s {{m|Stoked Sparksurfer}} and [[Pikachu in a cap]]'s {{m|10,000,000 Volt Thunderbolt}}.
* Thunderbolt's Japanese name, ÔľĎÔľź„Āĺ„āď„Éú„Éę„Éą ''100,000 Volts'', corresponds with the information present in some of Raichu's [[Pok√©dex entry|Pok√©dex entries]], which state its electric bursts can reach and, in some cases, even exceed 100,000 volts.
* Several moves have Japanese names in the same format, referencing a number of volts.
** In [[Pok√©mon Adventures]], {{adv|Red}}'s Pikachu [[Pika]] used an improvised move called '''[[Moves not in the Pok√©mon games#Pok√©mon Adventures|MegaVolt]]''' (Japanese: '''ÔľĎÔľźÔľź„Āĺ„āď„Éú„Éę„Éą''' ''1,000,000 Volts''), whose measurement of 10<sup>6</sup> volts puts it at 10 times the strength of Thunderbolt.
** {{m|10,000,000 Volt Thunderbolt}} (Japanese: '''ÔľĎÔľźÔľźÔľź„Āĺ„āď„Éú„Éę„Éą''' ''10,000,000 volts''), the exclusive Z-Move of [[Pikachu in a cap]], has a measurement of 10<sup>7</sup> volts, being 100 times the voltage of Thunderbolt.
** Though not named in exactly the same format, {{m|Gigavolt Havoc}}'s (Japanese: '''„āĻ„ÉĎ„Éľ„ā≠„É≥„āį„āģ„ā¨„Éú„Éę„Éą''' ''Sparking Gigavolt'') name contains the {{wp|metric prefix}} "giga", which indicates 10<sup>9</sup> (1,000,000,000), making it 10,000 times the voltage of Thunderbolt.
* While Thunderbolt is depicted crashing down on the opponent in the handheld games, its depiction in the console games is more akin to how it is depicted in the animated series, as the bolt goes straight toward the opponent. Despite this change, its description still stated "a strong electric blast crashes down on the target" until [[Pok√©mon Legends: Arceus]].
* In {{game|Red and Green|s}}, the battle effect for Thunderbolt contains several more, shorter flashes compared to the animation in the international {{game|Red and Blue|s}}. The move animation was likely toned down to prevent {{wp|epileptic seizure}}s.
* In some copies of {{game|Ruby and Sapphire|s}}, using Thunderbolt or {{m|Thunder}} may {{DL|List of glitches in Generation III|Stuck audio bug|cause the game to freeze}} during the moves' animation sequences. This, however, can be prevented by turning off the [[battle effects]] in the options menu.
** In {{game2|FireRed|LeafGreen|Emerald}}, the sound of Thunderbolt or Thunder may get stuck until the game resets.
* Because it has been a staple of {{AP|Pikachu}}'s moveset since [[EP005|the fifth ever episode]], Thunderbolt is one of the most, if not ''the'' most, featured move throughout all of {{aniseries|PTS}}. In addition, as revealed in ''[[XY025|Climbing the Walls!]]'', it is {{Ash}}'s favorite move.
* Thunderbolt is among the three attacks enhanced into "new" moves in the [[Pok√©mon Adventures]] manga, the others being {{m|Razor Leaf}} and Thunder.
