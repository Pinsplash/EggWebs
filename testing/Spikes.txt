'''Spikes''' (Japanese: '''„Åæ„Åç„Å≥„Åó''' ''{{wp|makibishi|Caltrop}}'') is a non-damaging {{type|Ground}} [[move]] introduced in [[Generation II]].

It is:
* [[TR23]] in {{g|Sword and Shield}}.
* [[TM090]] in {{g|Scarlet and Violet}}.
* [[TM088]] in [[Pok√©mon Legends: Z-A]].

==Effect==
===Generation II===
Spikes sets an [[List of moves that cause entry hazards|entry hazard]] around the enemy team's field. Pok√©mon who switch into the spike-ridden field take 12.5% of their maximum HP in damage (rounded down). {{type|Flying}} Pok√©mon are immune to this damage. The move {{m|Rapid Spin}} removes Spikes around the user.

In [[Pok√©mon Stadium 2]], if both players are forced to switch-in their last remaining Pok√©mon, and they have 12.5% or less HP, then they receive Spikes damage, faint and the match is declared a draw.

====Spikes glitch====
In this generation only, Pok√©mon fainting from Spikes will not run the regular faint checks until later, meaning that player can potentially end up with a Pok√©mon with 0HP on the field which hasn't fainted if the game loop doesn't do any further unrelated faint checks later. The easiest way to reproduce this is to have a Pok√©mon faint to {{m|Perish Song}}, then switch into a Pok√©mon who immediately faints from Spikes.

===Generations III and IV===
Spikes can now be set up to three times, each increasing the damage done to the opponent upon switching in. One layer of Spikes will damage the opponent Pok√©mon by 1/8 (12.5%) of its maximum HP, two layers will deal 1/6 (16.67%), and three layers will deal 1/4 (25%). Any Pok√©mon with {{a|Levitate}} will also be immune to the damage.

The move {{m|Defog}} will remove Spikes around its target. Any Pok√©mon under the effect of {{m|Magnet Rise}} (if [[Baton Pass (move)|Baton Passed]]) or the {{a|Magic Guard}} Ability are unaffected by the damage. However, Flying and Levitating Pok√©mon are affected under {{m|Gravity}}, while holding an [[Iron Ball]], or if Baton Passed an {{m|Ingrain}}.

===Generation V===
Spikes can now be reflected with {{m|Magic Coat}} and the Ability {{a|Magic Bounce}}. Any Pok√©mon holding an [[Air Balloon]] will be immune to the damage.

===Generation VI onwards===
{{m|Defog}} now removes all Spikes from play regardless of which side of the field they were placed on.

Spikes can be used as part of a [[Contest combination|Contest Spectacular combination]], causing {{m|Circle Throw}}, {{m|Dragon Tail}}, {{m|Roar}}, and {{m|Whirlwind}} to give an extra three appeal points if used in the next turn.

If powered up by a [[Groundium Z]] into Z-Spikes, the user's {{stat|Defense}} stat rises by one stage.

===Generation VIII===
====Pokemon Sword and Shield====
If a Pok√©mon is holding [[Heavy-Duty Boots]], they are immune to Spikes when entering battle. 
====Pok√©mon Legends: Arceus====
In [[Pok√©mon Legends: Arceus]], Spikes is a [[physical move]] that deals direct damage with a base power of 40. It also leaves the target with {{status|splinters}} for 4 turns ([[Move mastery|Strong Style]]), 2 turns ([[Move mastery|Agile Style]]), or 3 turns (otherwise).
