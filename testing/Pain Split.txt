'''Pain Split''' (Japanese: '''„ÅÑ„Åü„Åø„Çè„Åë''' ''{{jwp|Áóõ„ÅøÂàÜ„Åë|Pain Split}}'') is a non-damaging {{type|Normal}} [[move]] introduced in [[Generation II]]. Prior to [[Generation IV]], it was the [[signature move]] of {{p|Misdreavus}}, but other Pok√©mon could still inherit it via {{pkmn|breeding}}. It is [[TM202]] in {{g|Scarlet and Violet}}.

==Effect==
===Generation II===
Pain Split adds the current [[HP]] of the user and target Pok√©mon. It then divides this value by two, rounds down if necessary, and increases or decreases the HP of each Pok√©mon to become equal to the result (limited by each Pok√©mon's maximum HP). This setting of HP is not subject to effects such as {{m|Counter}} or {{m|Bide}}. Pain Split does not employ {{DL|Type|type effectiveness}}, so it can also affect {{type|Ghost}} Pok√©mon.

Pain Split has an [[accuracy]] of 100.

Pain Split fails if the target is behind a {{OBP|substitute|doll}}.

===Generation III===
Pain Split now bypasses {{stat|accuracy}} checks to always hit, unless the target is in the [[semi-invulnerable turn]] of a move such as {{m|Dig}} or {{m|Fly}}.

In Generation III only, a loss of HP due to Pain Split contributes to the accumulated damage of {{m|Bide}}; it still is not subject to other effects such as {{m|Counter}}.

Pain Split can be used as the second move of a [[Contest combination|Pok√©mon Contest combination]], with the user gaining 1 bonus appeal point if {{m|Endure}} was used in the previous turn.

===Generation IV onwards===
Pain Split can no longer contribute to the accumulated damage of {{m|Bide}}.

It is also part of a [[Contest combination|Contest Spectacular combination]] and will give an extra three appeal points if the move {{m|Endure}} was used in the prior turn.

If powered up by a [[Normalium Z]] into Z-Pain Split, the user's {{stat|Defense}} stat rises by one stage.

=== Generation VIII ===
Pain Split averages the two Pok√©mon's non-Dynamax HP, calculates the signed difference between that average and each Pok√©mon's non-Dynamax HP, then changes each Pok√©mon's HP by that signed difference.

==Trivia==
* Pain Split's Japanese name is a phrase that is specifically a {{wp|sumo}} wrestling term used formerly in the rule, which refers to calling a draw on a match because one player is too injured to continue.
* In [[Generation III]], the status screen erroneously states that the move has 100% accuracy, even though it ignores accuracy and evasion checks.
* If {{p|Shedinja}} uses Pain Split (via {{m|Mimic}}), it will cut the target's [[HP]] in half, similarly to {{m|Super Fang}}.
